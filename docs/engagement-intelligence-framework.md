# Codistan Engagement Intelligence Framework

## Purpose

This framework governs two automated decision points:

1. **Before first outreach** — perform a complete qualification and research audit, decide whether the lead is worth contacting, choose the right Codistan offer, and produce an engagement-focused first message.
2. **After every reply** — classify the response, identify what the prospect actually wants, recommend the next commercial action, and draft a formal response for the assigned team member.

The system provides guidance. High-risk replies involving pricing commitments, legal terms, security claims, contractual promises, discounts, or delivery guarantees always require human approval.

## A. Lead qualification and first-outreach audit

### Hard gates

A lead is blocked from automatic outreach when any of the following is true:

- company is in an excluded country or industry
- official company presence cannot be verified
- no legitimate business contact route exists
- the company has opted out or is already suppressed
- the reason for outreach is unsupported or stale
- no Codistan service can be credibly matched
- the message would require an unverified claim

Blocked leads are marked for research, human review, nurture, or rejection.

### Research record

For every lead, retain:

- official website and active business evidence
- company description, geography, industry, and size indicators
- recent trigger or current business signal
- likely buyer role and decision authority
- public contact source and confidence
- likely operational or commercial need
- Codistan service match
- strongest relevant portfolio proof
- expected objection or risk
- research date and source links

Never present an inferred need as a confirmed requirement.

### Qualification score

Score each lead out of 100:

| Dimension | Weight | What is evaluated |
|---|---:|---|
| Strategic service fit | 20 | Clear match to a Codistan service and delivery model |
| Verified demand or trigger | 15 | Recent hiring, launch, expansion, contract, partnership, product gap, or live requirement |
| Buyer quality and authority | 10 | Relevant founder, executive, department head, product, technology, marketing, or procurement role |
| Commercial capacity | 15 | Funding, revenue evidence, enterprise clients, procurement activity, paid product, growth, or high-ticket operations |
| Timing and urgency | 10 | Current initiative, deadline, active hiring, launch window, procurement date, or stated requirement |
| Portfolio and proof match | 10 | Strong and specific Codistan case study or capability |
| Contact quality | 5 | Verified business email or strong official route |
| Personalisation depth | 5 | At least one specific, supportable observation |
| Partnership potential | 5 | Recurring delivery, white-label, outsourcing, referral, or channel value |
| Risk and compliance | 5 | Geography, industry, reputation, privacy, suppression, and claim safety |

### Qualification outcome

- **80–100: Priority** — eligible for automatic first-outreach preparation.
- **68–79: Qualified** — eligible when mandatory evidence is complete.
- **55–67: Human review** — research gaps or weaker commercial fit.
- **Below 55: Nurture or reject** — do not contact automatically.

A strong micro-company can qualify despite low headcount when it has at least two credible commercial signals.

### First-outreach strategy

The first message uses this structure:

1. **Verified observation** — one specific, current, supportable point.
2. **Business implication** — why that point may create a delivery, growth, capacity, or technical need.
3. **Relevant Codistan offer** — one service or delivery model only.
4. **Proof** — one short and relevant credibility point.
5. **Low-friction CTA** — ask permission for examples, a brief fit check, or a short conversation.

### First-outreach quality rules

- normally 65–110 words
- plain, professional, and specific
- no false familiarity
- no fake “Re:” or “Fwd:” subject
- no unsupported hiring, budget, or buying claim
- no long company introduction
- no generic list of all Codistan services
- no attachment in the first email unless explicitly required
- one clear CTA
- subject line normally 3–7 words
- assigned sender’s approved signature is added at send time

### Required first-outreach output

The audit returns:

- qualification score and decision
- confidence level
- evidence summary and source links
- missing information
- buyer hypothesis
- likely need hypothesis
- recommended service
- recommended proof asset
- message angle
- 2–3 subject options
- final first-email draft
- preferred sender
- reply-to owner
- follow-up angle
- compliance warnings
- next action

## B. Reply post-processing framework

### Reply classifications

Every inbound reply receives one primary intent:

- `positive_interest`
- `meeting_request`
- `request_for_information`
- `pricing_or_budget_question`
- `technical_or_capability_question`
- `referral_to_another_person`
- `not_now`
- `existing_vendor_or_internal_team`
- `budget_objection`
- `not_relevant`
- `unsubscribe_or_stop`
- `automatic_reply_or_out_of_office`
- `bounce_or_delivery_failure`
- `ambiguous`

Also record sentiment, urgency, decision authority, explicit questions, objections, requested material, buying-signal strength, and risk level.

### Reply handling rules

#### Positive interest

Acknowledge the exact point raised, answer the immediate question, and move toward a short discovery call or a defined next step without overselling.

#### Meeting request

Confirm availability or ask for suitable times, provide a concise agenda, and involve the assigned lead owner.

#### Request for information

Answer directly, share only relevant proof, and ask one useful discovery question.

#### Pricing or budget question

Do not invent pricing. Ask the minimum scope questions needed, share only approved ranges, and require human approval.

#### Technical or capability question

Answer within verified Codistan capability, state assumptions and limitations, involve a technical owner when required, and require review for unsupported or high-risk claims.

#### Referral

Thank the sender, ask for an introduction or contact details politely, and reassign the lead when the new contact is confirmed.

#### Not now

Acknowledge timing, ask permission to reconnect at a specific future period, and schedule a follow-up only when permission is clear.

#### Existing vendor or internal team

Do not challenge the arrangement. Position Codistan as overflow, specialist, white-label, or backup capacity only when relevant.

#### Budget objection

Acknowledge the constraint and explore smaller scope, pilot, or phased delivery without immediately discounting.

#### Not relevant

Thank them and close politely. Stop the sequence.

#### Unsubscribe or stop

Suppress immediately, stop all follow-ups, and record the request.

#### Automatic reply or out of office

Do not treat it as a prospect response. Preserve the current pipeline status and use the stated return date when available.

#### Bounce

Stop sending to that address, mark the contact invalid, and research another legitimate route.

#### Ambiguous

Do not assume interest. Draft a short clarification and require human review.

### Required reply-analysis output

For every reply, produce:

- primary classification
- confidence
- short summary
- positive and negative signals
- explicit questions
- objections
- inferred intent, clearly marked as inference
- recommended pipeline status
- recommended next action
- suggested owner
- suggested response draft
- materials to share
- meeting agenda, when applicable
- follow-up date or stop instruction
- whether human approval is mandatory
- risk notes

### Human-approval triggers

A response is never auto-sent when it contains or requires:

- pricing, discount, refund, payment, or commercial commitment
- delivery timeline or staffing guarantee
- legal, contractual, data-protection, or compliance claim
- security architecture or penetration-testing assurance
- reference to a confidential client or project
- negotiation, complaint, threat, dispute, or reputational risk
- ambiguous intent with low confidence
- custom proposal or statement of work

## C. Learning loop

Track outcomes by source, service category, company type, trigger type, buyer role, sender, first-message angle, subject line, proof asset, reply classification, objection, positive reply, meeting, proposal, win, and reason lost.

Do not optimise for open rate. Optimise primarily for positive replies, qualified meetings, proposals, and won work.

Confirmed outcomes feed back into qualification weights, source priority, outreach wording, proof selection, and follow-up timing.
